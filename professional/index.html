About

I am a Biomedical Engineering MSc graduate from ETH Zurich, with my thesis completed at Harvard Medical School's Brigham and Women's Hospital. I build automated, production-quality computational systems — from end-to-end medical imaging pipelines processing hundreds of CT scans, to multi-task ML models predicting glucose from physiological wearable data.

I am drawn to hard quantitative problems that require turning noisy, high-dimensional data into structured, reliable outputs. My work spans medical AI, signal processing, and clinical decision support; I am equally interested in applications in data engineering, quantitative research, and computational modelling more broadly. I am Italian, British, and based in Switzerland.

Signal processing and coherence analysis on EMG and force-plate (CoP) data from 100 participants (50 elderly, 50 young). Developed MATLAB pipelines for EMG–EMG and EMG–CoP coherence analysis to quantify the effect of subthreshold auditory white noise on fall-risk in elderly individuals.
